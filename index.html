Aerodynamics Research

Vortex Induced Vibrations in Idling Wind Turbine Blades

  • Received a strong acceptance by International Congress of Sound & Vibration on the first attempt with unanimous reviewer scores of 10/10.
  • Designed an end-to-end aeroelastic–CFD–ML pipeline for vortex-induced vibrations in 15 MW wind turbine blades using dynamic meshing with k–ω SST turbulence closure.
  • Integrated high-fidelity CFD with a Gaussian Process Regressor achieving ~0.8% RMSE for near-exact stress prediction over sparse sensor data.
  • Derived a pitch control strategy that reduced peak flap-wise stresses by ~20%, demonstrating direct structural load mitigation for utility-scale turbines.
  • Conducted tuning-factor (Λ) resonance analysis to identify critical aeroelastic lock-in regimes under operational wind conditions.
